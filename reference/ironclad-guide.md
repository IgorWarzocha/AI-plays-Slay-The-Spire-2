# Ironclad Decision Guide for Current STS2 Data

This guide is based on the live local reference library at `runtime/reference-library.json`, not STS1 memory. It is meant to answer "what should I usually pick?" for Ironclad when drafting cards, choosing events, and evaluating relics.

## What Is Different From STS1

- `Paper Phrog` is much stronger because this pool has a deep `Vulnerable` package: `Bash`, `Break`, `Tremble`, `Thunderclap`, `Uppercut`, `Taunt`, `Molten Fist`, `Dismantle`, `Bully`, `Dominate`, `Vicious`, and `Cruelty` all care directly about `Vulnerable`.
- Ironclad has a real self-damage package now. `Bloodletting`, `Hemokinesis`, `Breakthrough`, `Brand`, `Offering`, `Inferno`, `Spite`, `Rupture`, `Tear Asunder`, `Demon Tongue`, `Self-Forming Clay`, and `Red Skull` all point in that direction.
- Exhaust is deeper than old Ironclad. `Ashen Strike`, `Forgotten Ritual`, `Pact's End`, `Stoke`, and `Howl from Beyond` give more reasons to convert exhaust from "cleanup" into a main scaling plan.
- Some cards are not STS1-equivalent role players. `Demonic Shield` and `Tank` are ally-facing cards, so they are much lower priority in solo runs. `Corruption` is an `Ancient` build-around, not something to force blindly.

## Default Draft Plan

If no clear build is open yet, draft in this order:

1. Reliable `Vulnerable`.
2. Card flow and energy.
3. Premium scaling package.
4. AoE.
5. Deck-thinning events and removals.

In practice, that means early rewards should usually prefer cards like `Thunderclap`, `Tremble`, `Uppercut`, `Shrug It Off`, `Pommel Strike`, `Headbutt`, `Burning Pact`, `Bloodletting`, `Battle Trance`, `Offering`, `Flame Barrier`, and `Whirlwind` over slow payoff cards with no shell.

## Best Packages

### 1. Vulnerable Burst

This is the most reliable default Ironclad lane in the current pool.

Core enablers:

- `Thunderclap`
- `Tremble`
- `Uppercut`
- `Bash`
- `Taunt`

Best payoffs:

- `Paper Phrog`
- `Cruelty`
- `Dismantle`
- `Bully`
- `Dominate`
- `Molten Fist`
- `Break`

Selection rule:

- If the deck already has 2 strong `Vulnerable` applications, aggressively prioritize payoff attacks and relics over more setup.
- `Paper Phrog` is a premium pickup and often reason enough to lean harder into this lane immediately.
- `Molten Fist` is much better than it looks because doubling `Vulnerable` turns all later burst cards on.

### 2. Exhaust Value

This is the best scaling shell when your deck starts to bloat or you pick up exhaust relic support.

Core engine:

- `Burning Pact`
- `True Grit`
- `Second Wind`
- `Feel No Pain`
- `Dark Embrace`

Best payoffs:

- `Fiend Fire`
- `Ashen Strike`
- `Forgotten Ritual`
- `Pact's End`
- `Stoke`
- `Charon's Ashes`
- `Forgotten Soul`

Selection rule:

- Do not draft payoff first unless it is independently playable. `Fiend Fire` is fine early; `Pact's End` is not.
- Once you have both `Feel No Pain` and `Dark Embrace`, exhaust cards become premium, not filler.
- `Corruption` is strongest when the deck already has real skill density plus one of `Feel No Pain` or `Dark Embrace`.

### 3. Self-Damage Strength

This shell is real and much better than old "just Hemokinesis sometimes."

Core enablers:

- `Rupture`
- `Bloodletting`
- `Hemokinesis`
- `Offering`
- `Brand`
- `Inferno`

Best payoffs:

- `Tear Asunder`
- `Spite`
- `Fight Me!`
- `Red Skull`
- `Self-Forming Clay`
- `Demon Tongue`
- `Ruined Helmet`

Selection rule:

- `Rupture` is a signal card. If you already have cheap self-damage, take it aggressively.
- `Demon Tongue` is exceptional here because it turns the first self-hit on your turn into effectively free scaling.
- `Ruined Helmet` sharply raises the value of `Inflame`, `Brand`, `Fight Me!`, `Dominate`, `Setup Strike`, and even the first `Rupture` trigger each combat.

### 4. Block Conversion

This package is good when the offered cards push you defensive instead of explosive.

Core cards:

- `Shrug It Off`
- `Flame Barrier`
- `True Grit`
- `Impervious`
- `Stone Armor`
- `Unmovable`

Payoffs:

- `Body Slam`
- `Grapple`
- `Juggernaut`
- `Barricade`
- `Toric Toughness`

Selection rule:

- `Barricade` is not an early blind pick. It is great after you already have dense block generation or `Unmovable`.
- `Body Slam` becomes premium only after you can regularly make 15+ block in one turn.
- `Juggernaut` improves a lot if the deck also contains repeated block sources like `Rage`, `Crimson Mantle`, `Stone Armor`, or `Toric Toughness`.

### 5. Strike Package

This package is playable, but only when it is clearly open.

Core cards:

- `Perfected Strike`
- `Pommel Strike`
- `Twin Strike`
- `Setup Strike`
- `Hellraiser`

Best support:

- `Headbutt`
- `One-Two Punch`
- `Ultimate Strike`
- `Paper Phrog`
- Strength relics and cards

Selection rule:

- Do not take `Perfected Strike` and then keep removing `Strike` cards out of habit.
- If you commit to this lane, remove `Defend` first and keep `Pommel Strike`, `Twin Strike`, `Setup Strike`, `Ashen Strike`, and `Ultimate Strike`.
- `Hellraiser` is strong only when your deck is still dense with cards containing `"Strike"`.

## Remove, Transform, and Upgrade Priorities

### Removes

Default rule:

- Remove `Strike` first.

Exceptions:

- Remove `Defend` first if you are committed to `Perfected Strike` or `Hellraiser`.
- Remove curses before basics unless the curse is temporary or exhaust-friendly and the basic card is actively ruining your deck.

### Transforms

Default rule:

- Transform `Strike` first.

Exceptions:

- Transform `Defend` first in the strike package.
- In a strong block shell with `Body Slam` or `Unmovable`, bad attacks can be better transform targets than `Defend`.

### Best Upgrades

Highest impact:

- `Offering`
- `Dark Embrace`
- `Barricade`
- `Body Slam`
- `Pyre`
- `Infernal Blade`
- `Havoc`
- `Stampede`
- `Battle Trance`
- `Bloodletting`

Very strong when relevant:

- `Whirlwind`
- `Fiend Fire`
- `Flame Barrier`
- `Tremble`
- `Thunderclap`
- `Uppercut`
- `Rupture`

Low-value upgrades unless they fix a breakpoint:

- `Strike`
- `Defend`
- `Anger`
- `Iron Wave`

## Ironclad Relic Priorities

Top class relics:

- `Paper Phrog`
- `Ruined Helmet`
- `Charon's Ashes`

Very strong and frequently enabling:

- `Demon Tongue`
- `Self-Forming Clay`
- `Red Skull`

Situational:

- `Brimstone`

Notes:

- `Brimstone` is only correct when you can kill quickly or heavily outscale the enemy strength buff. It is much better in bursty vulnerable decks than in slow control decks.
- `Burning Blood` stays excellent because so many strong Ironclad lines spend HP for tempo or scaling.

## Event Choices That Matter Most

These are the decisions with the clearest Ironclad bias in the current event set.

### Usually Excellent

- `Dense Vegetation`: remove a card for `11 HP` if your life total is stable. Ironclad can repay HP better than most classes.
- `Doors of Light and Dark`: remove `1` over random upgrading `2` unless your deck is already clean and has many premium upgrade hits.
- `Morphic Grove`: transform `2` for `100 gold` when Act 1 basics are still clogging the deck.
- `Spirit Grafter`: remove a card over `Metamorphosis` unless you specifically want random free attacks for attack-count synergies.
- `Whispering Hollow`: transforming a card for `9 HP` is usually better than two random potions.
- `Zen Weaver`: pay for removes whenever your economy allows it. This is one of the cleanest Ironclad improvement events.

### Strong With Ironclad-Specific Context

- `Amalgamator`: take `Ultimate Strike` if you are on strike/strength/offense; take `Ultimate Defend` if the deck is block-focused or still dies to tempo loss.
- `Hungry for Mushrooms`: `Fragrant Mushroom` is usually better than `Big Mushroom`. Losing `2` opening draws every combat is a real cost; Ironclad usually prefers the random upgrades.
- `Luminous Choir`: remove `2` plus `Spore Mind` is acceptable only if your deck already has meaningful exhaust. Otherwise the `149 gold` relic option is safer.
- `Grave of the Forgotten`: `Forgotten Soul` is the safe default. The `Decay` plus `Soul's Power` line is only worth it when exhaust is already core and the target card is excellent.
- `Wood Carvings`: `Toric Toughness` is the better general pickup; `Peck` is better only when you already care about strength or repeated-hit attacks.
- `Tea Master`: buy the temporary strength buff only when you are about to fight important elites or the boss. Otherwise the cheaper opening-hand upgrade effect is a better short-term spend.

### Usually Avoid

- `Field of Man-Sized Holes`: avoid the `Normality` line almost always. Ironclad has explosive turns and hates the 3-card cap.
- `This or That?`: prefer the gold over a random relic plus `Clumsy` unless your deck is already happy to exhaust dead cards.
- `Wellspring`: random potion is usually safer than taking `Guilty` for one remove.
- `Unrest Site`: full heal plus `Poor Sleep` is risky because `Poor Sleep` retains and clogs future turns. Take it only if survival is otherwise in doubt.

## Drafting Rules That Hold Up

- Do not force a 3-cost power deck without energy. `Barricade`, `Demon Form`, `Howl from Beyond`, `Bludgeon`, and `Stomp` are not a plan by themselves.
- Do not over-draft payoff before setup. One `Vulnerable` card plus one payoff card is better than three payoff cards and no setup.
- Ironclad in this pool is rewarded for spending HP deliberately, not randomly. If the HP payment gives deck quality, tempo, or repeatable scaling, it is often correct.
- Exhaust is not just cleanup. When you already have the engine pieces, it becomes one of the strongest late-game plans available.
- If a deterministic remove or transform is competing against an enchant option with unclear output, prefer the deterministic line. The local library does not fully surface enchant effect text, so opaque enchant outcomes should not be over-valued.
