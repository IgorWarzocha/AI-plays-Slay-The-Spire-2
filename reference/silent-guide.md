# Silent Decision Guide for Current STS2 Data

This guide is based on `runtime/reference-library.json`, not STS1 memory. It is focused on the current STS2 Silent pool: what to draft, what to skip, what to remove, and which event or relic decisions matter most.

## What Is Different From STS1

- The `Weak` package is much more lethal. `Tracking` makes `Weak` enemies take double damage from Attacks, and `Paper Krane` pushes incoming damage reduction much harder than old Silent.
- `Poison` is not just attrition here. `Accelerant`, `Bubble Bubble`, `Corrosive Wave`, `Outbreak`, `Twisted Funnel`, and `Snecko Skull` make it a real burst-scaling shell.
- `Shiv` support is deeper. `Helical Dart`, `Ninja Scroll`, `Phantom Blades`, `Fan of Knives`, and `Knife Trap` turn Shivs into a real build instead of only filler damage.
- Several cards are weaker in solo than they look. `Flanking` and `Sneaky` are ally-facing. Treat them as low-priority solo picks.

## Default Draft Plan

If no clear archetype is open yet, draft in this order:

1. Draw and hand smoothing.
2. Defensive stability.
3. One real damage engine.
4. AoE.
5. Removes and clean transforms.

In practice, that means early rewards should usually prefer cards like `Acrobatics`, `Backflip`, `Leg Sweep`, `Blade Dance`, `Cloak and Dagger`, `Deadly Poison`, `Poisoned Stab`, `Dagger Spray`, `Footwork`, `Prepared`, and `Tools of the Trade` over slow payoff cards with no shell.

## Best Packages

### 1. Poison Control

This is the safest default Silent lane in the current pool.

Core enablers:

- `Deadly Poison`
- `Poisoned Stab`
- `Bouncing Flask`
- `Noxious Fumes`
- `Haze`

Best payoffs:

- `Bubble Bubble`
- `Accelerant`
- `Outbreak`
- `Corrosive Wave`
- `Mirage`
- `Snecko Skull`
- `Twisted Funnel`

Selection rule:

- Once the deck has two reliable poison sources, `Bubble Bubble` and `Accelerant` become premium pickups.
- `Twisted Funnel` and `Snecko Skull` are true build-shaping relics here.
- `Mirage` improves sharply once poison application is already dense.

### 2. Shiv Tempo

This is a real STS2 shell because the relic and payoff density is high.

Core enablers:

- `Blade Dance`
- `Cloak and Dagger`
- `Leading Strike`
- `Hidden Daggers`
- `Infinite Blades`

Best payoffs:

- `Accuracy`
- `Phantom Blades`
- `Fan of Knives`
- `Knife Trap`
- `Helical Dart`
- `Ninja Scroll`
- `Afterimage`

Selection rule:

- Do not force this lane without actual Shiv creation.
- `Helical Dart` is one of the best class relics when this shell is open because it turns every Shiv turn into scaling.
- `Fan of Knives` is much better when the deck can already flood the hand with Shivs.

### 3. Discard Value

This shell is stronger than old Silent because both offense and defense reward discard.

Core engine:

- `Acrobatics`
- `Prepared`
- `Dagger Throw`
- `Calculated Gamble`
- `Tools of the Trade`

Best payoffs:

- `Tingsha`
- `Tough Bandages`
- `Storm of Steel`
- `Memento Mori`
- `Shadow Step`
- `Tactician`
- `Reflex`

Selection rule:

- Do not take `Tactician` or `Reflex` before the deck can reliably discard them.
- `Tough Bandages` is one of the strongest relics in the class because it converts routine cycling into real defense.
- `Storm of Steel` becomes much stronger once discard relics are online.

### 4. Weak Attack Burst

This is the main reason not to underrate Silent's debuff package.

Core enablers:

- `Neutralize`
- `Sucker Punch`
- `Leg Sweep`
- `Malaise`
- `Suppress`

Best payoffs:

- `Tracking`
- `Paper Krane`
- `Assassinate`
- `Precise Cut`
- `Skewer`
- `The Hunt`

Selection rule:

- `Tracking` is a signal card. If you already apply `Weak` consistently, it changes the damage ceiling of the whole deck.
- `Paper Krane` is premium because it stabilizes fights while also making `Tracking` lines easier to set up.

## Remove, Transform, and Upgrade Priorities

### Removes

Default rule:

- Remove `Strike` first.

Exceptions:

- Remove `Defend` first only if you are already fully committed to hyper-aggressive Shiv or discard burst and your defense is overbuilt.
- Remove curses before basics unless the curse is temporary or the deck can reliably discard around it.

### Transforms

Default rule:

- Transform `Strike` first.

Exceptions:

- In a very stable poison-control deck, a bad leftover attack can be a better transform target than `Defend`.

### Best Upgrades

Highest impact:

- `Adrenaline`
- `Afterimage`
- `Blade Dance`
- `Leg Sweep`
- `Noxious Fumes`
- `Well-Laid Plans`
- `Phantom Blades`
- `Wraith Form`

Very strong when relevant:

- `Acrobatics`
- `Backflip`
- `Bouncing Flask`
- `Dagger Spray`
- `Tools of the Trade`
- `Tracking`
- `Skewer`

Low-value upgrades unless they fix a breakpoint:

- `Strike`
- `Defend`
- `Slice`
- `Deflect`

## Silent Relic Priorities

Top class relics:

- `Twisted Funnel`
- `Tough Bandages`
- `Paper Krane`

Very strong and frequently enabling:

- `Snecko Skull`
- `Helical Dart`
- `Tingsha`
- `Ninja Scroll`

Notes:

- `Twisted Funnel` is the cleanest poison enabler in the class.
- `Helical Dart` turns Shiv turns from tempo into scaling.
- `Ring of the Snake` remains excellent because Silent benefits from seeing setup pieces early.

## Event Choices That Matter Most

### Usually Excellent

- `Dense Vegetation`: remove a card for `11 HP` if your life total is stable. Silent usually repays the life by drawing cleaner turns.
- `Doors of Light and Dark`: remove `1` over random upgrading `2` unless the deck is already unusually clean.
- `Morphic Grove`: transform `2` for `100 gold` if basics are still clogging the deck.
- `Whispering Hollow`: transforming a card for `9 HP` is usually better than two random potions.
- `Zen Weaver`: premium remove event. Silent loves clean decks more than almost any class.

### Strong With Silent-Specific Context

- `Amalgamator`: `Ultimate Defend` is the better default; take `Ultimate Strike` only if your deck is already offense-heavy and cutting defense is safe.
- `Hungry for Mushrooms`: `Fragrant Mushroom` is usually better than `Big Mushroom`. Silent does not want to lose `2` opening draws every combat.
- `Tea Master`: the temporary starting-hand upgrade is strong when your deck is setup-heavy and wants key powers or draw cards online immediately.
- `Wood Carvings`: `Toric Toughness` is the safer general option; `Peck` is better only when you are already leaning aggressive and want cheaper frontload.
- `Luminous Choir`: the `Spore Mind` line is usually worse for Silent than for exhaust-friendly classes. The `149 gold` relic option is safer when affordable.

### Usually Avoid

- `Field of Man-Sized Holes`: avoid the `Normality` line almost always. Silent often wins by chaining many cheap cards in one turn.
- `This or That?`: prefer the gold over a random relic plus `Clumsy` unless the deck is already built to discard dead cards every cycle.
- `Wellspring`: random potion is usually safer than taking `Guilty` for one remove.

## Drafting Rules That Hold Up

- Do not draft all three of poison, shivs, and discard at once without a reason. Silent is strongest when one engine is clearly primary.
- Prioritize consistency first. Silent's best decks start by drawing and blocking well, then add the kill package.
- `Weak` is better than it looks in this pool because it scales both offense and defense through `Tracking` and `Paper Krane`.
- If a deterministic remove or transform is competing against an enchant line with unclear output, prefer the deterministic line.
