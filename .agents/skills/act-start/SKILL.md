## Core Doctrine

- Start each act by identifying what the deck is good at, what still kills it, and what the act is most likely to test next.
- Route in chunks, not node by node.
- Do not send the run into elites just because a new act begins with full healing or fresh optimism.
- Decide whether the act plan is to exploit current strength, patch a fatal weakness, or buy time for scaling to come online.
- Boss preparation starts immediately. The act boss is not a floor-16 problem.

## Act Start Checklist

- Reconstruct the real deck: draw quality, frontload, scaling, AoE, block, exhaust, potion support, and relic spikes.
- Identify the worst realistic fight in the act and whether the deck already has a plan for it.
- Mark which rewards or shops would genuinely change pathing permission.
- Decide how many elites the deck has earned right now, not in theory.
- Decide what must be fixed before the boss.
- Late in an act, do not over-preserve if the deck is already ahead of the act's tests and the route still has recovery or potion outs.
- A route with strong relics, upgraded control, spare potion capacity, and a fire before the boss often has earned one more elite even at imperfect HP.
- If the route is already forcing a hard fight, spend current HP, gold, and potion capacity to improve that fight instead of preserving fake safety into it.

## Decision Standard

- Prefer routes that preserve multiple outs.
- Avoid plans that require several future hits in sequence just to become reasonable.
- When two paths are close, choose the one that fits the current deck's tested strengths.
- If the act presents unknowns, favor lines that gather information without forcing high-risk commitments too early.
