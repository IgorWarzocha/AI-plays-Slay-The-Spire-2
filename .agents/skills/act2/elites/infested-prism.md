# Infested Prism

## What It Is

- Act 2 elite
- Closest useful mental model:
  - block-break threshold fight that turns into a strength race if you leak tempo

## Current Read

- `Vital Spark` is the first thing to read correctly: the energy refund is only earned when an attack actually gets through, not when it merely pings into block.
- The fight punishes fuzzy "setup plus maybe redraw fixes it" turns because its defend turns bank a large block cushion and its later attack turns scale into real HP-loss checks.
- Once the prism has stacked strength, medium-defense turns stop being acceptable. You need either a real defensive answer or a clean damage push that meaningfully shortens the fight.
- `Unmovable` plus improved `Defend`s can be strong here, but only when the turn can actually cash in the doubled block. Spending a setup turn and then failing to convert it is a major leak.
- The really costly misplay pattern was spending limited defensive resources on turns where the enemy was not attacking, then reaching the later heavy attack turns with those tools already gone.

## Practical Rule

- Break the enemy's block deliberately before assuming `Vital Spark` will refund energy.
- On attack turns, prioritize concrete survival math over speculative draw lines unless the draw is the only way to reach a real defensive threshold.
- Treat defend-plus-buff turns as permission to push damage only if the line does not make the next heavy attack turn collapse.
- If using `Unmovable`, decide the full defensive line first; do not spend the setup and then discover the actual block card cannot follow.
- Treat limited defensive resources as actual survival resources. If the enemy is not threatening this turn, do not spend one-shot or hard-to-replace defense casually unless the fight is ending or the exchange is clearly worth it for the next cycle.
- The real danger turn is the later multi-attack after strength has stacked. If the prism is still healthy by then, assume you may need a potion or a premium block card to stay alive.

## Failure Mode To Avoid

- assuming `Vital Spark` refunds through block and planning energy around a refund that never comes
- over-greedy `Burning Pact` / setup lines on turns where simple defense was already required
- burning `Unmovable` on a big hit turn without confirming the follow-up block card is actually playable
- treating the fight like a free vulnerable-damage race after one good turn when the prism is still building strength
- spending limited defense on low-pressure turns and then discovering the future high-pressure turn has no real defense left
- letting the prism survive into the second huge multi-attack because earlier defend or buff turns were spent on low-value setup instead of real damage

## Durable Lesson

- `Infested Prism` is not mainly a raw damage-check elite. It is a sequencing and threshold-check elite: break block cleanly, preserve limited defense for the turns that actually demand it, and do not drift into the late strength cycle unless you already have a real answer for the big multi-attack.
