# Entomancer

## What It Is

- Act 2 elite
- Closest useful STS1 mental model:
  - `Book of Stabbing` pressure plus `Sentries`-style deck pollution

## Current Read

- The visible attack number is only part of the danger. `Personal Hive` means every Attack that hits it adds `Dazed` into your draw pile, so weakly blocked multi-hit turns can poison the next cycle even if the current HP loss looks manageable.
- This makes the fight a status-race threshold check, not just a raw damage race.
- The buff turn is a real kill window. If you fail to convert that turn into meaningful damage, the next multi-attack arrives with more deck clutter and worse redraw quality.
- Compact damage is better than fussy chip lines here because each extra hit can carry an extra future tax.

## Practical Rule

- On attack turns, block for the next cycle too, not just the current life total.
- Prefer fewer, stronger hits over many low-value hits when the damage output is comparable.
- Treat the non-attacking buff turn as permission to push damage and shorten the fight.
- Do not spend scarce defense on the buff turn just because energy is left over; preserve it for the next real attack cycle unless lethal or a major breakpoint is on the table.

## Failure Mode To Avoid

- seeing only current-turn damage and ignoring the incoming `Dazed` tax
- taking low-impact attack lines that bloat the next shuffle more than they improve the board
- wasting the buff turn on cosmetic setup instead of shortening the fight
- arriving at the later multi-attack with both less HP and a worse deck than the turn before

## Durable Lesson

- `Entomancer` is best treated as a deck-pollution elite. The right question is not just "how much damage am I taking now?" but also "what will my next hand look like after this exchange?"
