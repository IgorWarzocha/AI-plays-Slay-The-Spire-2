# Pathing And Thresholds

Use this note when deciding how aggressive Act 2 should be.

## Core Rule

Act 2 routes are earned by repeatable survivability, not by one good relic or one healthy HP bar.

## Questions To Answer Before An Elite

- Can the deck survive the second dangerous turn, not just the first?
- Does it have a real answer to defend-plus-buff cycles or large multi-attacks?
- Are key defensive tools reusable, or are they limited and easy to run out of?
- Is potion capacity helping the route right now, or is it wasted?
- Does the post-elite path still have real recovery?

If those answers are weak, the elite is not earned yet.

## Act 2 Route Heuristics

- Early Act 2 shops are strongest when they decide elite permission clearly.
- Unknown rooms are good when the deck wants outs without paying hallway tax immediately, but they should not hide the fact that a later route chunk is still a hard check.
- Fires in Act 2 should be judged through the next elite or boss breakpoint, not through generic upgrade greed.
- Once the route is committing into a known hard cycle, spend current resources to solve that cycle instead of preserving fake safety.
- Around the first third of Act 2, do a scaling audit. If the deck still cannot describe its long-fight win condition, route toward shops, upgrades, or safer nodes instead of pretending another fair hallway reward will solve the boss.

## Hallway Damage Rule

Act 2 hallway fights are often real threshold checks in disguise.

If the deck is leaning on potion outs, limited defense, or one premium block card, normal fights can easily become the reason an elite or boss line stops being earned.

## Durable Lesson From Recent Runs

- Defend or buff enemy turns are often the real damage race. If the deck fails to convert those windows, the later attack cycle becomes much harder.
- Limited defense must be routed like a real resource, not a generic energy sink.
- A route that preserves potion capacity and one clean recovery node can earn an elite; a route that burns those outs beforehand usually has not.

## Living Rule

Keep this note strict. If an Act 2 pathing mistake repeats, rewrite the rule here.
