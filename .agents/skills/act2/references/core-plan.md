# Core Plan

Use this note for the big Act 2 question: what does the deck need to stop winning only when the draw order is kind?

## What Act 2 Actually Tests

- repeated high-damage turns instead of one clean opener
- whether defend-plus-buff enemy turns are converted into meaningful pressure
- whether the deck's defense is reusable or secretly limited
- whether draw quality and potion capacity can rescue bad cycles
- whether Act 1 strength was real or just enough for Act 1 numbers
- whether the deck has real scaling by the time early Act 2 rewards and shops stop being theoretical

## Draft Priorities

In most runs, Act 2 rewards this order:

1. real defensive turns
2. draw or energy smoothing that improves bad cycles
3. scaling or payoff that closes longer fights
4. efficient pressure into defend or buff turns
5. compactness and removal of dead medium cards

## Practical Rule

- A card is good in Act 2 if it improves the next few hard checks, not if it merely adds one more medium turn to the deck.
- Respect how defense is delivered. Reusable defense, burst defense, and limited defense are not interchangeable.
- Empty potion slots are part of deck weakness in Act 2, not harmless spare capacity.
- Broad heuristic: if the deck still has no real scaling by roughly one third of the way through Act 2, assume the boss matchup is already in trouble and draft, shop, and smith accordingly.
- By that point, stop congratulating the deck for winning fair fights. Ask whether it now has a way to win a long fight after one bad cycle, one tax turn, or one heal-plus-buff turn from the enemy.
- At roughly that same point, move into an upgrade-gated reward posture. Once the deck is "good enough" to function, unupgraded commons and other medium cards should mostly be skipped unless they are missing engine pieces or unusually high-impact role fixes.

## Failure Modes To Avoid

- acting like Act 1 frontload alone is enough for Act 2
- cashing in limited defense on low-pressure turns and then dying to the next real attack cycle
- taking a value route while pretending the deck has already solved long-fight survival
- over-trusting one strong turn instead of asking what the second and third dangerous turns look like
- ignoring deck-pollution pressure in status fights and evaluating them only through visible HP loss
- arriving at mid Act 2 with no real scaling, too many starter-quality cards, and no plan to fix that before the boss

## Living Rule

When repeated Act 2 draft or reward lessons show up, merge them here before they disappear into run logs.
