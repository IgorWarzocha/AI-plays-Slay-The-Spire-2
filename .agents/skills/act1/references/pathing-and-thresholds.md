# Pathing And Thresholds

Use this note when deciding how aggressive Act 1 should be.

## Core Rule

Act 1 routes are earned in chunks, not node by node.

## Questions To Answer Before An Elite

- Does the deck have enough frontload to avoid falling behind immediately?
- Can it survive a weak opening hand?
- Is there a potion, shop, or campfire that materially improves the check?
- Does the post-elite path still have recovery?

If those answers are weak, the elite is not earned yet.

## Act 1 Route Heuristics

- Early shop lines are strongest when they let the run stay disciplined, not when they justify greed.
- Unknown rooms are good when the deck wants information or deck-quality outs without locking into a hard fight too early.
- Campfires in Act 1 should be judged through the next real threshold, not through upgrade greed alone.
- Late Act 1 route choices should already be filtered through the boss.

## Hallway Damage Rule

Do not treat hallway fights like free space.

If the deck becomes setup-heavy or is carrying a narrow margin into the next elite or boss, normal fights become part of the threshold check.

## Durable Lesson From Recent Runs

- The first elite is good when the deck is ready, not when the icon is tempting.
- Act 1 value events often justify safer pathing rather than immediate aggression.
- Quiet damage leaks before the boss can matter more than one flashy decision later.

## Living Rule

Keep this note strict. If a pathing mistake repeats, rewrite the rule here.
