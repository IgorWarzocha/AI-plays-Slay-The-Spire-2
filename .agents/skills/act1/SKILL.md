## Core Doctrine

- Act 1 is where the run earns permission.
- Prioritize frontload, vulnerable, draw smoothing, and enough block to survive bad opens.
- Do not route into elites because the icon is there. Route into elites because the current deck, potions, and path recovery justify them.
- Hallway damage matters. Act 1 losses often begin in “easy” fights that quietly tax away elite or boss margin.
- Shops and events should sharpen the opening deck, not make the route greedier than the reward actually earned.
- Boss preparation starts immediately because the Act 1 boss often demands a specific fight texture, not generic good cards.

## Workflow

1. Confirm the boss and current floor.
2. Rebuild the real opening deck shape:
   - frontload
   - vulnerable access
   - AoE
   - draw/energy smoothing
   - weak turns and dead draws
3. Count how many elites the deck has earned right now.
4. Check whether the next shop, event, or fire changes that answer.
5. For known Act 1 hard fights, read the encounter file before committing.
6. After each meaningful Act 1 floor, promote new durable lessons into this skill instead of burying them in the general hard-fight notes.
