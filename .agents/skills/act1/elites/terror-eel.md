# Terror Eel

## What It Is

- Act 1 elite
- Closest useful mental model:
  - breakpoint fight

## Current Read

- The whole fight is about controlling the sub-50% breakpoint.
- Panic tempo is worse than staged damage if staged damage forces the stun or transition on the right turn.
- The stun is a major swing, but it is not the whole fight. After the breakpoint, the eel can still punish sloppy turns with heavy follow-up damage if you stop respecting block and sequencing.
- Multi-target or broad debuff tools like `Shockwave` help buy the exact turn structure the fight wants, but even plain Ironclad can win by staging damage carefully and preserving one real defensive turn around the post-stun cycle.

## Practical Rule

- Plan the damage break deliberately instead of jamming attacks as soon as possible.
- If a potion changes when the stun or breakpoint happens, that is often the right time to spend it.
- Keep enough defensive margin for the turn after the stun window; do not treat the first stun as automatic safety.

## Failure Mode To Avoid

- pushing the threshold at a sloppy time and then trying to recover from the bad cycle
- valuing raw current-turn damage over breakpoint control
- assuming the fight is solved once the stun has fired
