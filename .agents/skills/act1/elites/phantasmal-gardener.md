# Phantasmal Gardener

## What It Is

- Act 1 elite
- Closest useful mental model:
  - board-control fight with a first-hit tax

## Current Read

- The dangerous part is the wide opening board, not any one individual gardener.
- `Skittish` means the first hit on a target each turn leaves behind block, so damage spread across fresh enemies is often fake progress.
- The fight gets much easier once one body dies. Until then, the board can snowball through repeated attack turns and strength gain.
- Strong single-hit openers, `Vulnerable`, and disciplined block are worth more than just jamming multi-hit cards into fresh targets.

## Practical Rule

- Choose a kill target each turn instead of spraying damage.
- Sequence with `Skittish` in mind:
  - use setup or one strong opener first
  - then use follow-up damage to finish through the granted block
- Respect defensive turns while the board is still wide; once the first kill happens, convert immediately into cleanup.

## Failure Mode To Avoid

- spreading attacks across untouched gardeners and calling it progress
- using multi-hit attacks as first contact into `Skittish` when a single-hit setup line was available
- panic-batching through kill uncertainty on a board where target removal changes the whole turn

## Durable Lesson

- Treat this as a board-simplification elite, not a raw damage race. The important threshold is the first clean kill.
