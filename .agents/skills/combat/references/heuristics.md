# Heuristics

Use this as the default tactical note for ordinary combat turns.

## Core Rule

Play for position, not just for current-turn efficiency.

## Setup Threshold

Setup is correct when it materially improves:

- next-turn survival
- the next shuffle
- scaling before the fight snowballs
- hand quality under status pressure

Do not reject setup just because an attack line spends energy cleanly.

Playing a power is often also deck-thinning. In longer fights, a drafted power that stays unplayed is not neutral; it is a recurring dead draw that keeps taxing future hands until it is finally cast.

## Tempo Vs Position

Before committing a line, ask:

- am I solving the real threat
- am I improving the next cycle
- am I spending HP to buy a stronger future turn, or just leaking it
- am I turning a recurring dead draw into live board text while the window is still available

## Card Draw Is Life

- Card draw is not luxury text. In many fights it is defense, damage, and future energy all at once because it preserves options.
- Hand size can itself be survivability when relics, powers, retention, or end-of-turn effects care about cards left in hand.
- A draw debuff is often more than "one fewer card." If the deck needs to spend energy on draw cards to stay functional, losing passive draw can also become an energy tax.
- Cheap repeatable cards and draw cards should be treated as part of the deck's life support in longer fights. Do not casually exhaust or trade them away unless the new position clearly justifies it.
- When two lines are close, prefer the one that preserves future card quality over the one that only wins the current energy-use contest.

## Chip Damage Is Not Always Bad

- Not every point of chip damage must be fully covered this turn.
- Small intentional HP loss can be correct when it preserves card draw, setup, a key cheap card, or a much stronger next-cycle position.
- The question is not "did I take damage" but "what did the damage buy?"
- Distinguish acceptable chip from compounding taxes. Repeating HP loss, scaling enemy damage, or chip that forces future weak turns is often much more expensive than it looks.
- If a line spends extra cards or energy only to avoid trivial damage while making the next cycle worse, that line is usually fake safety.

## Patience

- Do not spend all energy just because it is available.
- If the fight is asking for patience, take the patience line.
- If the fight is asking for a race, identify the real breakpoint first.

## Potion Timing

- Potions are for breakpoint turns, not average smoothing.
- Spend them when they change setup, survival, or lethal math.
- In multi-body fights, a potion that removes or nearly removes a body can be worth more than a higher raw-damage potion used later.
- Do not spend a potion just because the enemy is alive. If the enemy is not threatening this turn, the potion should usually need to change the next dangerous cycle, a phase breakpoint, or a clean kill.
- Do not turn an already-winning line into a potion line. If the fight is under control without the potion, keep the potion.
- Solve the whole turn before acting. Count energy, zero-cost cards, and potion outs as part of the line instead of discovering at end turn that clean value was left unused.

## Cost Display And Re-Reads

After any of the following, stop and re-read the state:

- card draw burst
- cost mutation
- free-card effect
- generated temporary cards
- target board mutation
- triggered draw from a power, relic, or status interaction

Do not batch actions through state changes.

## Threat Priority

- Identify the source of future damage, scaling, or deck pollution first.
- In multi-target fights, damage that does not simplify the active board can be fake progress.

## Resource Discipline

- HP is there to buy winning positions, not lazy lines.
- Preserve future outs when two lines are close.
- Treat temporary fight cards as mechanic text if the encounter makes them matter.
- A small current-turn damage gain does not justify leaving a meaningful power stranded in the deck if the fight is long enough for that power to matter.
- When a power was drafted for this kind of fight, failing to cast it in a safe or semi-safe window is usually not caution; it is a positioning mistake.
- Likewise, a small current-turn HP save does not automatically justify sacrificing future draw quality, hand size, or a key cheap damage card.

## Status And Junk

- Status clutter changes hand quality and can change the whole fight.
- A board that looks stabilized may still be lethal if the hand is clogged and the fight is not actually over.

## Living Rule

Keep this note short and sharp. When a repeated tactical lesson appears, merge it into an existing section instead of growing a pile of micro-notes.
