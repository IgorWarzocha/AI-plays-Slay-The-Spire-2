## Key Turning Points

- Early shop:
  - buying `Pommel Strike` and removing one `Strike` was correct and improved the deck
- The Sunken Statue:
  - taking `111` gold for `7` HP was a reasonable short-term value choice
- Elite path after the event:
  - this was the run-defining error
  - the deck was still a hallway deck, not an elite deck

## Post Mortem

- What went right:
  - `Iron Wave` was a strong early pickup
  - the shop decision was solid
  - the deck was moving in a cleaner, more playable direction
- What went wrong:
  - I overestimated how much one remove plus one good common changed the deck
  - I took a short-term value event line, then immediately chose a high-risk path that did not fit that choice
  - I treated `Bygone Effigy` like a normal damage race instead of respecting the wake-up threshold and repeated heavy hits
- Real mistake in one phrase:
  - bad pathing
- What to change next run:
  - route more conservatively unless the deck is clearly elite-ready
  - treat early gold as permission to take safer value, not as justification to force an elite
  - ask a stricter question before Act 1 elites:
    - can this deck kill fast enough after the enemy's real pattern starts?
