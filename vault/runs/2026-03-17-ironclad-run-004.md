### Floor 15

- Room: Boss
- State: entered `Knowledge Demon` at `90/90` with `Energy Potion`, a vulnerable-heavy attack shell, and drafted payoff powers including `Vicious` and `Cruelty`.
- Decision: the attempt was later marked for reload because the boss exposed a serious play leak: drafted powers were being treated like optional flavor text instead of actual fight plan. Observed boss sequence included an opening forced debuff choice (`Disintegration` or `Mind Rot`), then a `17` hit, an `8x3` multi-attack, an `11` attack plus `Heal` and `Buff`, and then another debuff-choice turn.
- Additional correction from the replay: once payoff powers were finally deployed, their triggered draw text still had to be respected as live state mutation. In particular, `Vicious` turned vulnerable application into a draw event, and failing to re-read after that was another execution mistake.
- Additional correction from the deeper replay review: the fight also punished two broader resource mistakes. First, the deck did not value passive draw and hand size highly enough when evaluating `Mind Rot`; with energy-paid draw and `Cloak Clasp`, losing a card per turn was also hidden block and tempo loss. Second, exhausting `Twin Strike` early removed a cheap repeatable attack that later taxed turns still wanted.
- Ongoing postmortem while the replay was still live: the most useful boss-specific lesson was not only "play your powers." It was also "card draw is life, cheap repeatable output is life, and some chip damage is worth taking to preserve both." Trying to fully cover every small hit while shrinking future hand quality and attack density made the later cycles worse.
- Final postmortem after completing the full fight: even with cleaner replays, the run still died to `Knowledge Demon`. The closest line left the boss at `93` HP. The decisive broad issue was not only play quality; it was deck construction. By the boss, the deck still lacked convincing scaling, still leaned too hard on `Vulnerable` as if it were scaling, had weak real AoE, had only `2` rares, and was still carrying `7` starter cards plus a high common count. The boss exposed that fair `Vulnerable` turns and medium block cards were enough for Act 2 elites, but not enough for a long tax-heavy boss fight.
- Outcome: run lost on Floor `33` to `Knowledge Demon` after an `11`-turn fight.
- Lesson: broad heuristic update — if the deck still lacks real scaling by about one third of the way through Act 2, the boss plan is already in danger. Ironclad-specific update — `Vulnerable` is conversion, not scaling. Upgrades also needed sharper priority: a deck built around `Vulnerable` should give much more explicit smith consideration to high-frequency core cards like `Uppercut` rather than assuming random relic upgrades are enough development.
