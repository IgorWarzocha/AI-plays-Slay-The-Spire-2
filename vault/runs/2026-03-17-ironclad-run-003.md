# Run: Ironclad 003

## During The Run

### Floor 0

- Room: Run start
- State: fresh modded session after defeat, aiming for a cleaner Act 1 with stricter pathing discipline
- Decision: took `Pomander` at Neow and used it on `Bash`
- Outcome: started the run with `Burning Blood`, `Pomander`, and `Bash+`
- Lesson: low-variance frontload is a better start for a win attempt than gambling on a random rare or paying max HP for transforms
- Links:
  - [[strategy/pathing-and-planning]]
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 1

- Room: Monster
- State: opened on `Sludge Spinner` with `Bash+` in hand and chose the center route to preserve an early shop line without forcing an early elite
- Decision: played `Bash+` into `Strike`, then took `Uppercut` from the reward
- Outcome: won the fight at `67/80`, gained `12` gold, and added a second premium debuff attack
- Lesson: the route discipline already paid off here; I got a strong hallway reward without spending the run on an elite check the deck had not earned yet
- Links:
  - [[strategy/pathing-and-planning]]
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 2

- Room: Monster
- State: the deck already had `Bash+` and `Uppercut`, so the next hallway fight was a chance to add either more frontload or a utility miss
- Decision: took `Headbutt` over the weaker alternatives after the fight
- Outcome: the deck picked up a clean damage-plus-recursion card that plays well with vulnerable turns and future upgrades
- Lesson: early Ironclad still wants compact, concrete cards; `Headbutt` keeps pressure high without diluting the plan
- Links:
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 3

- Room: Merchant
- State: entered the shop with enough gold to make a real power purchase and no need to bloat the deck with filler
- Decision: bought `Shockwave`
- Outcome: the run picked up a premium scaling/debuff card that materially improves elite and boss fights
- Lesson: this was the kind of shop where quality mattered more than quantity; one great card was better than multiple average ones
- Links:
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 4

- Room: Event
- State: hit `Brain Leech` after the shop with a deck that still wanted stronger combat play, not greedier pathing
- Decision: chose `Share Knowledge` and took `Taunt`
- Outcome: added another useful combat card without paying away the route discipline earned earlier
- Lesson: event value is best when it strengthens the current plan rather than pulling the deck sideways
- Links:
  - [[strategy/pathing-and-planning]]

### Floor 5

- Room: Elite
- State: this time the elite was actually earned, because the deck already had `Bash+`, `Uppercut`, `Headbutt`, `Shockwave`, and `Taunt`
- Decision: fought `Skulking Colony`, then took `Strike Dummy` and `Twin Strike`
- Outcome: won the elite, gained `42` gold, and turned the strike package into a real lane instead of dead starter filler
- Lesson: the contrast with the previous failed run is clear; elite pathing is good when the deck is ready, not when the icon is tempting
- Links:
  - [[strategy/pathing-and-planning]]
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 6

- Room: Rest Site
- State: after the elite reward, the deck had a strong immediate upgrade target in `Twin Strike`
- Decision: upgraded `Twin Strike`
- Outcome: locked in more frontloaded damage for the strike plan and improved hallway consistency
- Lesson: once `Strike Dummy` is online, upgrading the right strike card can be a better tempo gain than a greedier choice
- Links:
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 7

- Room: Monster
- State: entered `Corpse Slug` with `50/80` HP, `84` gold, and a deck that now has both debuff setup and strike payoffs
- Decision: opened by focusing one slug with `Bash` and `Twin Strike` instead of splitting damage
- Outcome: killed the first slug immediately, survived the follow-up line cleanly, finished the floor at `49/80`, gained `20` gold, and took `Iron Wave`
- Lesson: against linked enemies, concentrate damage first and solve the special power second
- Links:
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 8

- Room: Event
- State: `Doors of Light and Dark` offered either random upgrades or a single remove, so the real question was whether the deck still had enough dead weight to justify a precise cut
- Decision: took the `Dark Door` and removed a `Defend`
- Outcome: the deck dropped to `15` cards and kept the `Strike Dummy` attack package intact while trimming a low-impact block card
- Lesson: in this run state, a targeted remove beat random upgrades because the deck already had several strong upgraded or premium cards and still wanted tighter draws more than variance
- Links:
  - [[strategy/pathing-and-planning]]
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 9

- Room: Treasure
- State: arrived at the chest with a compacting deck, solid HP, and enough momentum that the relic mattered more as a plan-shaper than as a bailout
- Decision: opened the chest and took `Petrified Toad`
- Outcome: gained `Petrified Toad` and jumped from `104` to `152` gold, which makes the next shop materially more powerful
- Lesson: even when the relic text is new, the surrounding state still matters; here the gold swing was almost as important as the relic itself because it opens stronger future routing decisions
- Links:
  - [[strategy/pathing-and-planning]]

### Floor 10

- Room: Event
- State: `Drowning Beacon` offered a potion or a relic for max HP, and this was a good chance to force potion state into the run without paying permanent health
- Decision: took `Bottle`
- Outcome: obtained `Glowwater Potion` and kept max HP intact
- Lesson: when the run also needs new automation coverage, a line that creates a useful test surface can be the right gameplay pick if it is not meaningfully weaker
- Links:
  - [[strategy/pathing-and-planning]]

### Floor 11

- Room: Elite
- State: entered `Terror Eel` at `49/80` with `Glowwater Potion`, a free `Potion-Shaped Rock`, and a deck strong enough to plan around the stun breakpoint instead of mashing face damage
- Decision: spent the rock immediately, opened on `Shockwave`, then used a staged damage line to push the eel under 50% exactly when it mattered
- Outcome: beat the elite cleanly, preserved `Glowwater Potion`, took `Eternal Feather`, `Swift Potion`, `40` gold, and added `Feed`
- Lesson: breakpoint fights reward planning, not panic tempo; forcing the stun on a controlled turn was the whole fight
- Links:
  - [[strategy/pathing-and-planning]]
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 12

- Room: Rest Site
- State: arrived after the elite at `42/80`, then immediately healed to `51/80` from `Eternal Feather`, which changed the site from a recovery node into a development node
- Decision: took `Smith` and upgraded `Shockwave`
- Outcome: the deck now has a premium upgraded debuff card for upcoming hallway fights and the boss, while HP is still in a comfortable range
- Lesson: relic context matters; `Eternal Feather` effectively bought the smith by converting entry HP into free healing
- Links:
  - [[strategy/pathing-and-planning]]
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 13

- Room: Monster
- State: the `Unknown` rolled into `Calcified Cultist` plus `Toadpole`, which created a clean test for the new potion API and a real `Feed` lethal line
- Decision: used the free `Potion-Shaped Rock` to set up `Feed` on the `Toadpole`, then cleaned up the cultist with strike damage
- Outcome: gained `+3` max HP from `Feed`, finished at `60/83`, took `20` gold, and drafted `Setup Strike`
- Lesson: once `Feed` is in the deck, even simple hallway fights should be viewed through the lens of “can this be converted into permanent max HP?”
- Links:
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 14

- Room: Elite
- State: the route forced a late Act 1 elite before the final rest, so the decision was not whether to fight but how to spend limited HP and potions without panicking
- Decision: beat `Phantasmal Gardener`, then took `Amethyst Aubergine`, `Swift Potion`, `44` gold, and drafted `Unrelenting`
- Outcome: survived the elite, improved immediate boss damage, and converted the reward screen into a meaningful economy spike instead of a blind click-through
- Lesson: even late in the act, reward quality matters as much as fight execution; concrete text on relics and cards changes the pick, not just the confidence
- Links:
  - [[strategy/pathing-and-planning]]
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]

### Floor 15

- Room: Rest Site
- State: arrived at `36/86` after `Eternal Feather` healing, with one boss node left and enough deck quality that the real question was survival, not greed
- Decision: rested instead of smithing
- Outcome: healed to `61/86` and entered the boss with real margin instead of pretending one more upgrade would matter more than HP
- Lesson: late-act campfires are where pathing discipline cashes out; if the route already demanded extra risk, the campfire should often pay it back
- Links:
  - [[strategy/pathing-and-planning]]

### Floor 16

- Room: Boss
- State: entered `Decimillipede` with `Lantern`, `Oddly Smooth Stone`, `Mummified Hand`, and a free `Potion-Shaped Rock`, so the opening hand could afford real setup if it also removed a segment
- Decision: opened on `Vicious+`, used the `Mummified Hand` proc to fire a free `Bash+`, then spent the rock plus `Setup Strike`, `Feed`, and `True Grit` to kill the middle segment immediately
- Outcome: took one segment off the board on turn 1, finished the opener at `65/92` with `8` block, and reduced the fight from three live bodies to two
- Lesson: this is a `Darklings`-style board-control fight, not a normal damage race; the value was removing a live segment, not `Feed`, because segment deaths do not count as true encounter kills while other bodies remain
- Links:
  - [[strategy/combat-heuristics]]
  - [[strategy/ironclad-core-plan]]
  - [[encounters/hive/decimillipede]]

### Boss Post Mortem: The Insatiable

- Room: Boss
- State: arrived with strong debuff support, `Mummified Hand`, two `Potion-Shaped Rock`s, and enough frontload that the real question was reading fight windows correctly
- Decision: played the fight as a windowed boss, using buff turns to extend `Vulnerable` and cash damage, while treating `Frantic Escape` as real fight text instead of disposable clutter
- Outcome: killed `The Insatiable`, took `110` gold total from the boss rewards, and added `Demon Form`
- Lesson: the durable rule was “identify the window before spending energy.” Once the turn was classified correctly, the fight stopped looking exotic and turned into a disciplined damage-conversion check
- Links:
  - [[encounters/hive/the-insatiable]]
  - [[strategy/combat-heuristics]]

- State: entered the event with `Mummified Hand`, a real power package (`Vicious+`, `Pyre+`, `Demon Form`), `Strike Dummy`, and enough deck quality that the right generated card depended on synergy more than raw standalone text
- Decision: reloaded the event after the first instinctive `Weapon` lean, then took `Gadget -> Curious`
- Outcome: added a generated power that fits the run's relic context instead of just taking the most immediately legible attack frame
- Lesson: generated-card events must be judged against the current relic stack and deck engine, not by the most eye-catching preview
