## Post Mortem

### What Worked

- The early run was much better disciplined than the previous failed attempts.
- Pathing in early Act 1 was correct:
  - the first elite was taken only after the deck had actually earned it
  - the route preserved shop and recovery value instead of forcing low-quality risk
- The deck found a coherent lane:
  - strong debuff package (`Bash+`, `Uppercut`, `Shockwave+`)
  - strike scaling (`Strike Dummy`, `Twin Strike+`, later `Perfected Strike+`)
  - real power support (`Vicious+`, `Pyre+`, `Demon Form+`, `Mummified Hand`)
- Several individual fight reads were good:
  - `Terror Eel` was played as a breakpoint fight
  - `Decimillipede` was eventually identified correctly as a `Darklings`-style board-control elite
  - the Queen fight was finally recognized as a `Collector` / `Reptomancer`-style boss where the add is the real clock
- The damage-control merchant logic was strong:
  - spend to survive the forced elite or boss path
  - fill potion slots
  - remove low-value filler
- The compendium floor history makes one thing very clear:
  - floors `1-33` were mostly a winning run
  - the structure only collapsed after the late hallway damage spike before the emergency shop

### What Did Not Work

- The late boss play was too local.
- Even after identifying the Queen fight correctly, execution still drifted back into short-term tempo and face damage.
- The biggest concrete mistake was not pivoting early enough from aggression into stabilization.
  - one turn earlier, `Armaments+` plus block was better than the aggressive line that was taken
  - by the time `Uppercut+ + Defend` appeared, that turn was already damage control rather than the true mistake
- Temporary fight state was sometimes read correctly but not respected correctly.
  - free-card windows
  - `Bound`
  - `Mummified Hand` cost changes
- There was still a bias toward “use the energy on visible damage” instead of asking what the next cycle would look like.
- The run also leaked too much HP in late normal fights.
  - floors `35`, `36`, and `37` alone cost `74` HP before healing
  - that turned the floor `38` merchant from a powerful choice point into a bailout node
  - by the time `Queen` started, the run no longer had the slack a setup deck wants

### Durable Lessons

- Good pathing created this run's strength. Bad late-fight sequencing wasted it.
- The run-history floor list shows the loss was two-stage:
  - first, too much late hallway damage
  - then, a boss fight that still failed to pivot from pressure into stabilization
- In new STS2 bosses and elites, the right question is not just “what kills me now?” but also:
  - what is the real clock?
  - what older STS1 fight does this resemble?
  - if I solve the clock, does the fight slow down?
- On the Queen fight specifically:
  - killing `Torch Head Amalgam` first was the correct read
  - once the add died, the board simplified exactly the way the hypothesis predicted
  - the remaining failure came from subsequent turn choices, not from the threat model
- Setup remains the biggest improvement area.
  - When the deck contains `Mummified Hand`, scaling powers, and vulnerable-draw payoffs, setup turns must be exploited more cleanly.

### What To Change Next Run

- Preserve the good pathing discipline from this run.
- Treat late-act hallway fights as seriously as elites once the deck becomes setup-heavy. They were the hidden source of this loss.
- Respect stabilization one turn earlier in boss fights.
- Keep using STS1 parallels, but force a sharper question:
  - is this a race, a setup fight, a board-control fight, or a breakpoint fight?
- When a hard fight exposes a quiet turn, spend it on the line that improves the next cycle, not the prettiest current-turn number.
