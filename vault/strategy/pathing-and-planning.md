# Pathing And Planning

General gameplay note distilled from live runs.

## Core Rule

- Do not evaluate one node at a time.
- Evaluate the whole route your deck is actually prepared to survive.

## Pathing Heuristic

- A route earns elites.
- HP alone does not mean the route is good.
- Gold alone does not mean the route is good.
- One or two decent pickups do not mean the route is good.

Before taking an elite path, check:

- does the deck have enough frontload
- does the deck have enough block to survive the bad turns
- does the deck have any real scaling
- does the route offer recovery after the elite
- am I taking this elite because it is correct, or because it is tempting

If those answers are weak, the route has not earned the elite yet.

## Planning Heuristic

- Do not optimize only for the current turn.
- Play for the next few turns, especially in elite fights.

Questions to ask during combat:

- what does this line make my next two turns look like
- am I spending energy, or am I building a winning position
- is setup stronger than short-term tempo here
- if I wake the enemy or trigger phase two, can this deck handle what happens next

## Common Failure Mode

- taking a short-term value line
- then immediately choosing a high-risk path that contradicts that value line

Example:

- take gold because the deck wants safer immediate value
- then route into an elite anyway

That is usually greed disguised as confidence.

## Application To Ironclad

- `Inflame` and similar setup cards are often underplayed when the line is judged only by current-turn efficiency.
- `Iron Wave` is not just tempo. It can be part of a multi-turn survival line.
- Early Act 1 elites should be treated as threshold checks, not generic damage races.
